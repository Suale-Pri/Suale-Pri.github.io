Norway, The country of white tipped mountains and fjords.

Norway

Everyone knows that Norway is known for its amazing mountains and fjords, breathtaking views, brown cheese and dry fish. However, out of all of these things when I was in Norway I did 2, ate brown cheese and dry fish (I know its extremely disappointing). The whole country is expensive, the prices are crazy compared to Germany for example one 0.33l bottle of pepsi is 3 to 4 euros. The prices are high but so is the pay if you live there. But all of that shouldnt make you not visit it for the mountains and fjords if you do like the nature.

What to do

On top of not hiking the mountains, I didn't even go to many places which is quite disappointing. However the places that I did visit are worth going to. For example the fashion outlet. Almost all the big companies have their own shops there, Nike, Adidas, Hilfiger, some kind of make up and womens stores that I have forgotten and Calvin Klein. The outlet is located in Oslo which makes it even easier to go to. Another place in Oslo worth looking for is the opera house. Its extremely well constructed and you can even climb to the roof. The sloped surface of the house makes it an elegant add on to the capital.

Road trip

Now I will admit, I have sneaked in some pictures from Sweden in here, but only because I would recommend doing a road trip to Sweden if you have the time, It doesnt take long and visiting 2 countries is always better than 1 right? Nordens Ark zoo park is close to the borded and I highly recommend going there. The place itself is 950 acres large. The animals are well fed, taken care of and they have a lot of space in their pens. The zoo was a 10/10 experience. You need to be ready to walk quite a lot though which is 100% worth it.
